HISTORY

The municipality's land area is 52.12 sq. km or 20.12 sq. miles, which is 1.09% of Bohol's total area. The population is 21,158 based on the 2020 Census, which is 1.52% of Bohol's total population or 0.26% of the Central Visayas region's overall population. With these figures, the population density is 406 inhabitants per sq. km or 1,052 inhabitants per sq. mile.

Population of Clarin

Clarin is located in the northeastern part of Bohol and is 61 km away from Tagbilaran City. It was established on January 31, 1919, and was originally named "Can-ogong" after an early settler. It was later changed to Clarin to honor the first Civil Governor of Bohol. During World War II, the central school in Clarin was used as a concentration camp by the Japanese.

History of Clarin

The town of Clarin is politically sub-divided into 24 barangays, to wit: Bacani, Bogtongbod, Bonbon, Bontud, Buacao, Buangan, Cabog, Caboy, Caluwasan, Candajec, Cantoyoc, Comaang, Danahao, Katipunan, Lajog, Mataub, Nahawan, Poblacion Centro, Poblacion Norte, Poblacion Sur, Tangaran, Tontunan, Tubod and Villaflor.

Clarin Barangays

The St. Michael the Archangel Parish Church of Clarin was established in 1922 by Archbishop Juan P. Gorordo, originally named Virgin of the Holy Rosary Parish. The church's patroness was later changed to St. Michael the Archangel, and an ivory image of him was brought by a Spanish missionary priest, which was later stolen in 1984 after an offer to buy it was declined.
